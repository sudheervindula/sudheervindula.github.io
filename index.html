Case Studies

Short case-style narratives you can reference in interviews — focused on the problem, approach, and impact.

Reporting · Automation

From ad-hoc Excel chaos to governed Power BI

Problem: Business users relied on manually updated Excel files with inconsistent logic and numbers for management reporting.

Approach: I interviewed key stakeholders, mapped their existing reports, and reverse-engineered the underlying logic into a central SQL model. I then built a star schema in Power BI, defined standardized DAX measures, and rolled out a suite of dashboards with row-level security.

Impact: Reduced manual effort by hundreds of hours annually, established single-source-of-truth KPIs, and improved trust in the numbers across finance and operations.

Churn · Retention

Making churn drivers visible to non-technical teams

Problem: Leadership knew churn was an issue but had no clear visibility into when and why customers were leaving.

Approach: I defined churn events and cohorts, built retention curves, and enriched the data with segments like usage behavior, ticket history, and plan type. I visualized this via Power BI, including drill-downs and filters tailored to marketing and CX teams.

Impact: Helped identify high-risk cohorts and behaviors (e.g., low-usage first 60 days), enabling targeted onboarding and retention campaigns.

Data Engineering · Reliability

Stabilizing a fragile data pipeline

Problem: Existing scripts regularly failed on high volume days, causing downstream reporting delays and manual fixes.

Approach: I refactored the pipeline into modular PySpark jobs with better partitioning, error handling, and logging. I introduced intermediate checkpoints, schema validation, and basic data quality metrics exposed to the analytics team.

Impact: Improved pipeline reliability, reduced fire drills for the data team, and gave stakeholders more confidence in daily reports.
